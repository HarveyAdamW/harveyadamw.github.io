Mint Curry Scallops

Here's a recipe for scallops in a mint curry sauce. I threw this together while trying to use up some mint leaves. The smell of the mint inspired me to add some lime juice. The mint plus lime idea spurred all the spices. While cooking, the smell just absolutely screamed to me to add some peanut butter into it. Turned out delicious enough for me to be quite proud of it! So here's the recipe!

Ingredients:
20 medium scallops
20 mint leaves chiffonade (i.e., chopped into strips)
1 whole medium lime
1.5 tbsp garlic powder
1 tsp smoked paprika
0.5 tbsp ground ginger
0.5 tbsp herbs de Provence
2 tsp black pepper
3 tbsp curry powder
2 tbsp peanut butter (preferably smooth)


Cooking:
1. Add 3 tbsp oil to pan.
2. Heat pan on medium-high heat until very hot.
3. Place scallops on pan, leaving ample space between each scallop.
4. Salt scallops liberally.
5. Sear until bottom is a dark golden brown (about 1-3 minutes).
6. Flip scallops onto other side (using tongs is easiest).
7. Salt scallops liberally on this side.
8. Sear until bottom is a medium golden brown and scallops are still fairly squishy (i.e., still raw in the center).
9. Reduce heat to low (just low enough that water would no longer evaporate quickly).
10. Sprinkle mint leaves uniformly across pan.
11. Cook until mint leaves have darkened in color and are very fragrant (about 1 minute).
12. Squeeze lime over mint and scallops evenly (this is in part to reduce the pan temperature further).
13. Add garlic powder, smoked paprika, ginger, herbs de Provence, black pepper, and curry powder.
14. Cook for about 1 minute.
15. Push scallops gently to one side of the pan.
16. Add peanut butter to pan away from the scallops and gently stir, until the peanut butter has been incorporated into a homogeneous paste.
17. Gently roll the scallops around the pan to coat in sauce.
18. Serve.

The Leah

This is a recipe for a spin on the Reuben. I named it after myself, obviously. But it also works nicely since Reuben was Leah's son, and the most well-known variation of the Reuben is the Rachel, who was Leah's sister. This sandwich replaces the corned beef with pastrami and switches out the Russian dressing with mustard (or a mix of mustard and horseradish). Being Jewish, it feels more Jewish to me, in-so-much as mustard just feels extra Jewish to me.

Ingredients:
2 slices of Jewish rye bread
2 thick slices of Swiss cheese
4 tsp stone ground mustard (or 1 tsp horseradish mixed with 3 tsp stone ground mustard)
0.5 cups sauerkraut
1/4 lb pastrami


Cooking:
1. Toast bread lightly.
2. Spread mustard on one side of each slice of bread
3. Add pastrami, sauerkraut, and cheese (in that order) to one of the slices of bread.
4. Put other slice on top (mustard side down, of course).
5. Broil sandwich in oven at medium or low heat, until top bread slice is golden brown and cheese is melted.
6. Eat! And be careful, this is (like most deli sandwiches) a hefty sandwich that can get pretty messy.
